RULES FOR REGISTERING BIRTH CERTIFICATE
To Be Read Before Filling The Form

1.To apply for a Birth Certificate, you must first register the birth with “panchayat/ municipal/Corporation / Health Department” in your area.
2.The birth has to be registered with the concerned local authorities within 21 days of its occurrence, by filling up the form prescribed by the registering authority or as per the given link below along with a certificate from the person or Medical Institution, who conducted delivery in the case of birth.
3.Beyond the prescribed time limit,
i) 21 to 30 Days – With penalty of Rs.100
ii) 30 days to 1 year - Written permission of the Officer prescribed and on payment of late fee of Rs. 200
iii) After 1 year - Any birth which has not been registered within one year of its occurrence shall be registered by an order of the Executive Magistrate not below the rank of a Revenue Divisional Officer and on payment of late fee of Rs. 500

RULES FOR REGISTERING DEATH CERTIFICATE
To Be Read Before Filling The Form

1. A fee of Rs. 25 has to be paid for each copy.
2.If the date of death is unknown or unspecified, the search fee for every year is Rs. 25(per copy).
3.If there is a delay in the registering of deaths, such as if it is less than one month, it can be acquired without penalty; more than a month and less than a year, a fee of Rs. 25 has to be paid for each copy; and if it is more than a year with the order of Magistrate it is Rs.50.
4.As per the rules of the court, a fee stamp of Rs.2 should be affixed in the application form.
